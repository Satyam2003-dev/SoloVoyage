Transportation Safety

How you get around is a huge part of your journey. These tips will help you navigate planes, trains, and taxis like a seasoned pro.

Arrive at the Airport Early

Rushing through airports increases stress and the likelihood of making safety mistakes or forgetting important items.

  • Plan for Delays: Arrive at least 3 hours early for international flights and 2 for domestic. This gives you a buffer for long lines or unexpected issues.
  • Reduce Stress: Use the extra time to relax, eat a proper meal, charge your devices, and double-check your documents. A calm traveler is an aware traveler.
  • Avoid Vulnerability: Scammers and thieves often target travelers who look stressed, rushed, and distracted. Don't be one of them.

Keep Valuables in Carry-On Luggage

Never put important or valuable items in checked baggage, which can be lost, stolen, or damaged.

  • Essential Carry-On Items: Your passport, all money and credit cards, prescription medications, phone, laptop, camera, and a copy of your documents must stay with you.
  • Assume the Worst: Always pack your carry-on as if your checked bag will be lost. Include a change of clothes and basic toiletries.
  • Secure Your Carry-On: Keep your bag with you at all times in the airport and on the plane. Never leave it unattended, even for a second.

Use Official Taxi Services Only

Avoid unmarked taxis, people who approach you inside the terminal offering rides, or vehicles that don't look like legitimate taxis.

  • Find the Official Stand: At airports and train stations, look for the official, marked taxi stand. Ignore solicitors who approach you directly.
  • Identify a Real Taxi: Look for proper licensing, a working meter, and official company names and numbers displayed on the vehicle.
  • Share Your Ride Details: Before getting in, take a quick photo of the license plate and driver's ID number and send it to a trusted contact.

Book Airport Transfers in Advance

Pre-arranged transportation is almost always safer than figuring out options when you arrive tired and disoriented.

  • Reputable Companies: Book through your hotel or a well-reviewed, official shuttle or car service company online before your trip.
  • Confirm Details: Get a confirmation number, the driver's contact info, and the exact pickup location. A legitimate service will often ask for your flight number to track delays.
  • Avoid Scams: Having a pre-booked ride helps you confidently bypass the often chaotic and scam-filled arrivals hall transportation scrum.

Keep Your Phone Charged During Travel

A charged phone is essential for navigation, communication, emergencies, and accessing important information.

  • Charge at Every Opportunity: Fully charge your devices before leaving for the airport. Use charging stations during layovers. Keep your portable power bank topped up.
  • Manage Battery Life: Use power-saving modes, dim your screen, and close unnecessary apps to conserve battery during long travel days.
  • Have Non-Tech Backups: Despite your best efforts, phones can fail. Have your hotel address and key phone numbers written down on paper as a fail-safe.

Don't Tell Strangers You're Traveling Alone

Keeping your solo travel status private reduces your vulnerability to scams, theft, and other dangers.

  • Prepare a Vague Story: When asked, have a simple, believable story. You are "meeting friends," "visiting family," or "my partner is arriving tomorrow."
  • Deflect Personal Questions: Be wary of anyone, including overly friendly taxi drivers or locals, who asks too many questions about your plans, where you are staying, or for how long.
  • Project Confidence: It's a small, white lie for your safety. State your story confidently and change the subject.

Keep Belongings With You at All Times

Never leave your bags, electronics, or valuables unattended, not even for a moment.

  • The Restroom Rule: When using a public restroom, take all of your belongings into the stall with you. Do not ask a stranger to watch your things.
  • Physical Contact: When sitting in a cafe or waiting area, keep your bag on your lap or loop a strap around your leg. Don't just place it on the floor or an empty chair beside you.
  • Be Aware of Distraction Theft: Be extra vigilant if someone creates a commotion or spills something on you; it's a common tactic for an accomplice to steal your bag while you're distracted.

Stay Alert During Layovers

Airports can be confusing, and exhaustion during a long layover can make you vulnerable.

  • Confirm Your Next Gate: As soon as you land, find a departure board and confirm the gate and time for your connecting flight. Gates can change.
  • Set Multiple Alarms: If you plan to rest or sleep, set multiple alarms on your phone and watch to ensure you don't miss your flight.
  • Stay in Secure Areas: Remain past the security checkpoint. The "airside" of an airport is generally safer than the "landside" public areas.

Drink Water and Eat Healthy During Travel

Travel dehydration and poor nutrition can severely affect your judgment, energy, and ability to handle stressful situations.

  • Hydration is Key: Airplane cabin air is extremely dry. Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your flight. Avoid excessive caffeine and alcohol, which are dehydrating.
  • Maintain Energy Levels: Eat proper meals to keep your blood sugar stable. Pack healthy snacks like nuts or protein bars for long travel days to avoid energy crashes.
  • Boost Your Immunity: Being tired and run-down makes you more susceptible to getting sick. Prioritizing hydration and nutrition helps keep your immune system strong.

Sleep Carefully on Long Flights/Trains

While rest is important, you must balance it with security, especially when surrounded by strangers.

  • Secure Your Valuables: Before you doze off, ensure your most important items (passport, wallet, phone) are secured in a money belt or in a zipped inside pocket, not in the seat-back pocket.
  • Use Your Bag as a Pillow: Use your carry-on bag or backpack as a pillow or footrest. The physical contact will make it more likely you'll wake up if someone tries to move it.
  • Choose Your Seat Wisely: A window seat is often more secure for sleeping, as you can lean against the wall and are less exposed to traffic in the aisle.
