The Complete Solo Travel Checklist

Use this checklist before and during your trip to ensure you've covered all your safety bases. Stay prepared, stay safe.

Before You Leave:

  • Research destination thoroughly: Check government advisories, local news, crime stats, and engage with online forums.
  • Master sharing your plans: Create detailed itinerary, share with trusted contacts, set regular check-in times, and establish clear code words.
  • Get comprehensive travel insurance: Ensure robust medical coverage, including emergency medical evacuation, and confirm activity coverage.
  • Document everything smart: Take photos of passport, visa, cards; email/cloud storage; make multiple physical copies kept separately.
  • Learn key local emergency phrases: Practice "Help," "Police," "Hospital," "Lost," "I don't speak [language]" phonetically.
  • Choose safe neighborhoods & accommodation: Read solo traveler reviews, prefer 24-hr front desks, and proactively avoid ground floor rooms.
  • Book your first night's accommodation & airport transfer well in advance.
  • Verify your passport expiration date (ensure 6+ months validity beyond your trip).
  • Register with your home country's embassy or consulate.
  • Strategically use a money belt or hidden pouch for your main funds and passport.
  • Pack a comprehensive personal first aid kit: Include all prescriptions, pain relievers, anti-diarrheal medicine, and basic wound care.
  • Download essential offline maps & translation apps to your phone.
  • Bring multiple portable phone chargers (power banks) and adapters.
  • Pack a whistle or personal safety alarm for emergencies.
  • Leave all expensive jewelry and flashy valuables securely at home.
  • Notify your bank and credit card companies of your travel dates and destinations.
  • Check the weather forecast meticulously and pack appropriate clothing that helps you blend in.
  • Practice packing light and smart to maintain agility and discretion.

Daily While Traveling:

  • Conduct a morning safety check and review your daily plan.
  • Consistently stay in well-lit, busy areas; actively avoid isolated or dark spots.
  • Always walk with confident body language; avoid looking overtly lost or distracted.
  • Keep your phone accessible but secure; avoid careless display or leaving it unattended.
  • Unwaveringly trust your gut feelings; immediately leave uncomfortable or suspicious situations.
  • Avoid wearing headphones or earbuds in unfamiliar or crowded places to maintain auditory awareness.
  • Research all public transportation routes thoroughly and choose to sit near drivers or conductors.
  • Keep all bags closed and positioned in front of your body in crowded environments.
  • Have transportation tickets or exact change ready before boarding.
  • Pay diligent attention to your stops to avoid getting lost in unfamiliar areas.
  • Use ATMs inside secure banks or hotel lobbies; never count money in public.
  • Carry small bills for daily transactions; divide your money and cards across multiple secure locations.
  • Utilize crossbody bags with robust zippers; never place valuables in back pockets.
  • Employ travel locks on luggage; take photos of your belongings for documentation.
  • Always use a VPN when connecting to any public Wi-Fi network.
  • Strictly limit alcohol consumption; never accept or leave drinks unattended with strangers.
  • Eat at busy, reputable restaurants; be extremely cautious with street food; always drink bottled water in questionable areas.
  • Meet new people only in public, well-populated places; always inform someone of your plans.
  • Refrain from sharing exact locations or explicitly stating you're traveling alone on social media.
  • Turn off location services for social apps; use private messaging for sensitive communications.
  • Maintain regular check-ins with your trusted contacts back home.
  • Perform an evening safety assessment before going out or settling in for the night.

Emergency Preparedness:

  • Have local emergency numbers (police, ambulance, fire) readily accessible and memorized.
  • Know the contact information and address of your nearest embassy or consulate.
  • Keep emergency cash hidden in multiple, discreet locations.
  • Ensure backup communication methods (e.g., secondary SIM, Wi-Fi calling app) are available.
  • Familiarize yourself with the location of the nearest hospital and police station.
  • Practice staying calm in emergencies; ask for help from local authorities.
  • Do not be afraid to make noise (yell, use alarm) or physically remove yourself from dangerous situations.
  • Immediately call someone you trust to inform them of your situation.
